THE RESIGNATION OF THE PM
After nearly 3 gruelling years, our PM, Boris Johnson, has finally resigned after the government made him resign on the grounds that he was ‘abusing power’. Unexpectedly, the vote of no confidence to confirm this is expected on Wednesday without any parliamentary time. A Labour spokesman said “Yet again the Tories are changing the rules to protect their own dodgy mates. All the Tory leadership candidates should denounce flagrant abuse of power to protect their own prime minister”, in response to this. However, on Monday, the newly established 1922 Committee confirmed that he would be staying in office until the 5th November. Naturally, the government is arguing that the vote is a waste of time as Boris has ‘already resigned’. Keir Starmer also said that it would be ‘intolerable to Britain’ if they let him ‘cling on until the 5th'. Since his resignation was announced, Boris has been appointing a number of unusual successors to the position, and is intending hand out resignation knighthoods and peerages to allies, examples being culture secretary Nathan Dorries, and, supposedly, his own father. To further expand upon this, Boris’s wife, Carrie Johnson said that 10 Downing Street felt ‘like a prison’. His sister, Rachel Johnson, also said farewell to her brother being PM, saying that Boris ‘wanted to become world king at two, and actually almost achieved that’ during her speech.
MONKEYPOX GOES BANANAS
Monkeypox is a DNA virus. 18 new cases of monkeypox have been found in England by the UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA), and one additional case has been found in Scotland. As of June 7th, 321 people have been confirmed in the UK as of this point. 305 confirmed cases have been reported so far, with 11 cases in Scotland, 2 cases in Northern Ireland, and 3 cases in Wales. Most cases that have been reported to date have involved males who have intercourse with other men, so even while this advice is applicable to everyone, we are urging these individuals to be aware of the signs, especially if they have recently had a new sexual partner. If you develop a blistered rash, you should call NHS 111 or a sexual health centre right away if you: had spent the last three weeks in close proximity to someone who has or may have monkeypox (even if they have not been tested), had recently returned from a trip to West or Central Africa, a man who engages in male sex. Dr Ruth Milton, Senior Medical Advisor at UKHSA said: “Monkeypox is a DNA virus. The majority of cases that have been reported to date have involved gay, bisexual, and males who have intercourse with other men, so even while this advise is applicable to everyone, we are urging these individuals to be aware of the signs, especially if they have recently had a new sexual partner. If you develop a blistered rash, you should call NHS 111 or a sexual health centre right away if you: had spent the last three weeks in close proximity to someone who has or may have monkeypox (even if they have not been tested), had recently returned from a trip to West or Central Africa, a man who engages in male sex.” Health protection teams from the UKHSA are contacting individuals who are thought to be high-risk contacts of cases that have been confirmed and are encouraging those who have undergone risk assessments and are in good health to isolate at home for up to 21 days. In order to lower the likelihood of symptomatic infection and severe disease, UKHSA has additionally ordered more than 20,000 doses of the safe smallpox vaccine Imvanex (provided by Bavarian Nordic). This is being distributed to identified close contacts of persons who have been diagnosed with monkeypox.
